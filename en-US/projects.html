Link for WhatsApp

In Brazil, WhatsApp is the most used app by far, and there are many apps that use the official API to create links for WhatsApp that guide users directly to a conversation. But, the main problem with those tools is that many of them contain lots of ads or even subscriptions plans to create "premium WhatsApp links".

Com este projeto, criado para suprir a minha necessidade de popular designs de projetos que estava criando, você pode facilmente gerar um texto em latim personalizando o tamanho para atender às suas necessidades. O Gerador de Lorem Ipsum foi criado com a mesma base do Link de WhatsApp.

The project started with the creation of and interactive wireframe for the website in order to test the layout and features quickly. I used Framer to create the wireframes for both PC and mobile.

At first, I created the main blocks of construction: geometric forms that would set the layout of the website setted in grayscale to set the importance of each element. After that, content was inserted into each of those blocks. Then, features, like input forms, popups and buttons taken place. To finished the first part of the project, a color pallet and the icons were added and.

After the first step concluded, it was time to experiment with different ideas. Because the wireframe was interactive, creating, deleting and changing its features was easy and quickly I could test all of my ideas of interactivity.

With the intent to be easy-to-use, responsiveness was the top-one priority and the website was adapted to work on both small mobile devices and large monitors. Beyond responsiveness, interactivity was a priority too, and, because of that, the code was created to be as clean as it can be and easy to update, with many little interactions that help the user use the tool in a natural and expected way.

The advantage of being constructed by a multistep process gives the website an excellent technical SEO that was built from the scratch with it. The result of such planning and execution gave the website a +90 score on PageSpeed Insights for mobile devices and a +99 score for PCs and laptops, turning itself into a technical success project.

Dr. André website

The Dr. André's website was created with the objective to promote online schedules for consultations. Dr. André is a well-known ophthalmologist in the city of Porto Velho, in the North of Brazil, and, to build up his website, I had a 3-steps plan that unfolded in less than a month:

  1. Create a website for the doctor, so we could manage his digital presence and promoting campaings online.
  2. Create a WhatsApp Business account and set it up, so he could talk to his patients (in Brazil, WhatsApp is the top one app for communication).
  3. Create a continuous search advertising campaign to promote his website.

With that in mind, the path was decided: The website was built with the more up-to-date concepts of the HTML 5 semantic, CSS composition and JavaScript (ECMASCRIPT 6).

By the time his website was done and ready for deployment, I started linking the page with his new WhatsApp Business account. Down below you can see a screenshot of the WhatsApp profile. As you can see, the profile has pretty much the same information that the doctors website. It was very handy, because a lot of people just go straight to the WhatsApp, without looking any information available before in the website.

And with all set, we deploy the website and the new WhatsApp Business profile with a continuous search campaign on Google Ads, so we could boost the first interaction with the website and analyze what people do on it: The website has a properly set up in Google Analytics and uses Google Tag Manager to manage tags and fire tags in actions taken that are important to follow.

My Business School's Rebranding

In March 2021, the professor and coordinator of my Business School, Luiz Pinheiro, came up with the idea of a challenge for our class: Make a logo rebranding for the Business School. Me and some of my classmates accepted the challenge and we started to discuss about what we were going to do, how it will be done, and when we will need help to do so.

Our Brand Management professor, Fabiana Mariutti, joined the challenge with us as our guide. And just like that, between March and August of 2021, we completed the duty of making a Rebranding of our Business School.

We were guided by some thoughts like: "How to describe our Business School?" and "What kind of people do us wanted to target with that change?". By the middle of the project we finally chose a main thought, that was proposed by our guide professor: "We have to see our Business School as the shine start of our University" (adaptation of the original phrase: "A Business School como Prodígio da Universidade Positivo").

After ups and downs, with two pitches and a lot of work and discussion, two of our prototypes were chosen and, on September 25th, we presented our work to our classmates, professors and coordination with unanimous approval.

Artistic Intervention

The Artistic Intervention was a project that took place in 2019, during the last year of my High School. This project was the first project of the type in our school and the concept and realization was totally done by our class. We want to leave a mark in the classroom that we study and improve the environment.

After the coordination approved our idea, we started to organize funding for the project and to buy the items in order to make it happen. I was the one responsible for counting and managing the money, and, to do that, I had created a Coda document with all the information about how, where and when the money was spent. The document is still online and it's public now. For the people who helped us, recipes were made and sent to them to help manage everything at every point of funding.

After just one month, we had enough money to start the project, buying the equipment, tints and everything that we needed. All the costs were also in that initial document and recipes of our orders as well. For every order, the recipe was sent to the people that helped us fund the project, to keep them up to date about our progress.

We have a closed album of photos of our project for the founders, with all our progress during the time of the project. We also have a poll about what people think about our initiative and progress. This form was created by me and you can see it here.

In fact, our project was so big that even the school's marketing team used our work to publicize the school, but we always remember the people that were us that thought, planned and made the project. We are very pride about our achievements with this project and the mark that we left in the school.
